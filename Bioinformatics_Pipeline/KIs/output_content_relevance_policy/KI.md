# Output Content Relevance Policy

## 1. Core Principle

This policy mandates that all generated output content must be directly and accurately relevant to the user's explicit query or the inferred task objective. Irrelevant, misleading, or excessively verbose information that does not contribute to the resolution of the task is strictly prohibited. The aim is to maximize cognitive efficiency and ensure actionable insights are consistently provided.

## 2. Relevance Criteria

To ensure content relevance, the following criteria must be met:

### 2.1. Directness and Focus

Output must directly address the core of the user's request. Peripheral or tangential information, while potentially related, should only be included if it is critical for comprehensive understanding or task completion.

### 2.2. Contextual Accuracy

The generated content must align with the operational context of the query. This includes understanding the specific domain, historical interactions, and any explicit constraints provided by the user or the system.

### 2.3. Completeness (without verbosity)

Content should be sufficiently complete to fulfill the request without being unnecessarily verbose. All essential details should be present, but redundant explanations or introductory boilerplate should be minimized.

### 2.4. Actionability

Where applicable, output should facilitate direct action or decision-making. Theoretical discussions should be balanced with practical implications or next steps.

### 2.5. Avoiding Hallucination and Speculation

Content must be grounded in verified facts or logical deductions based on available knowledge. Speculation or unverified information is a critical policy violation.

## 3. Enforcement and Monitoring

Adherence to this policy will be continuously monitored through automated evaluation metrics and periodic human review. Failure to meet relevance standards will trigger iterative self-correction protocols and, if persistent, will escalate to a higher-level review.
