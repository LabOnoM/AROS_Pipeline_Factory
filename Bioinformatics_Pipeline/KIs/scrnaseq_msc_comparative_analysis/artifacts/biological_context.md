# Biological Context: BM vs. Cultured MSCs

## Population Shifts
- **Bone Marrow Resident**: Characterized by *Cxcl12*, *Lepr*, *Kitl*, *Igfbp4* (Niche population).
- **Cultured**: Shift towards a **fibroblastic** and **proliferative** state. 
- **Transcriptional Separation**: Culture expansion acts as a "transcriptional erasure" of $in vivo$ identity. Without batch correction, BM and Cultured populations form completely distinct clusters on UMAP, indicating that the culture process overwrites the majority of the original niche identity.
- **Losses**: Hematopoietic, erythroid (*Hba-a1*, *Car2*), endothelial (*Pecam1*, *Emcn*), and smooth muscle (*Myh11*) populations typically do not survive MSC culture expansion.

## Signature Genes
- **Cultured Upregulation**: *Actb* (cytoskeletal), *Timp1* (ECM remodeling), *S100a6* (stress/calcium), *Lgals1* (immunomodulation).
- **BM/Niche Presence**: *Cxcl12*, *Lepr*, *Igfbp4*, *Kitl*.

## Key Pathways
- **Culture Stress/Adaptation**: HIF-1 survival signaling, metabolic reprogramming (glycolysis shift), serine metabolism.
- **Niche Identity**: Pseudoachondroplasia/ECM structural proteins, muscle contraction (perivascular identity).
